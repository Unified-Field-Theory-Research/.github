# Unified Field Theory Research

This organization is an AI-assisted research workspace for investigating
whether spacetime-like geometry, and eventually more of the structure needed
for a unified field theory, can be recovered from finite-capacity
causal-network models.

The goal is not to announce that a unified field theory has been found. The
goal is to build a rigorous, inspectable research pipeline for attempting one:
clear hypotheses, explicit proof obligations, reproducible code, formal or
fail-closed certificates, and external review gates before any claim is
promoted.

## What We Are Attempting

The working idea is that smooth spacetime should be treated as something to
recover in a coarse-grained regime, not something silently assumed at the
microscopic level.

The current research direction asks:

- Can finite-capacity causal networks support spacetime-like geometry?
- Can intrinsic observables recover dimension, causal cones, isotropy, and
  metric-like behavior without importing a hidden continuum background?
- Can AI-assisted workflows help organize theorem ladders, proof obligations,
  simulations, audits, manuscripts, and review packets well enough to make the
  work externally checkable?
- If the geometry program survives review, what additional gates would be
  needed for curvature, dynamics, matter fields, gauge structure, quantum
  behavior, and eventually a unified-field-theory candidate?

## Methodology

This project treats AI as a research accelerator, not as proof authority.
AI-assisted drafts, conjectures, code, reviews, and theorem scaffolds are only
useful when they are backed by artifacts that can be checked independently.

The current methodology emphasizes:

- **Explicit claim boundaries:** every repo states what is proved, what is
  conditional, what is blocked, and what is out of scope.
- **Proof-obligation tracking:** theorem targets are broken into named
  obligations before any promotion is allowed.
- **Rust-native implementation:** simulations, validators, theorem gates,
  summaries, and audit tooling are kept in Rust unless a repo explicitly says
  otherwise.
- **Formal or fail-closed gates:** Lean certificates or equivalent fail-closed
  audits are used to prevent unresolved assumptions from being presented as
  results.
- **No-hidden-continuum audits:** geometry recovery work must avoid smuggling
  smooth spacetime into the microscopic premises.
- **Reproducibility and review:** manuscripts, artifact manifests, review
  packets, and external-evidence gates are part of the research process.

## Current Workspaces

The active repositories are public research workspaces. They are shared for
inspection, reproducibility, and external review, not as claims that the full
program has been proved.

- `finite-capacity-causal-geometry`: the core Paper 1 workspace for finite-capacity
  causal-network emergence. It is not a physical proof that nature uses this
  substrate.
- `higher-dimensional-geometry-recovery`: the downstream Paper 2 workspace for
  higher-dimensional geometry recovery. It is currently a non-promoting
  skeleton with open proof obligations.
- `.github`: the public organization profile and related coordination text.
- `unified-field-theory-codex`: the macro-level Codex workspace pattern for
  navigating the research repos together.

## Roadmap

The long-range plan is a paper ladder. Each paper is a conditional target, not
an announcement that the target has already been achieved.

1. **Internal Conditional Emergence Theorem:** maintain the core
   finite-capacity causal-network theorem target and its claim boundary.
2. **Higher-Dimensional Geometry Recovery:** define admissible
   higher-dimensional network classes and recover dimension, causal cones,
   isotropy, and metric-like observables without a hidden continuum premise.
3. **Curvature From Finite-Capacity Causal Networks:** define intrinsic
   curvature-like observables and test whether coarse-grained curvature can
   emerge from finite local structure.
4. **Einstein-Limit Or Gravitational Field-Law Recovery:** look for a narrow
   gravitational limit, such as Newtonian, weak-field, redshift, light-bending,
   or Einstein-like behavior in a stated regime.
5. **Quantum-Compatible Local Dynamics:** extend the update framework toward
   quantum-compatible local dynamics while preserving finite capacity,
   locality, bounded transfer, and no-signaling.
6. **Free Matter And Field Excitations:** test whether particle-like or
   field-like excitations arise from the substrate rather than being added as
   external labels.
7. **Gauge Symmetry From Local Redundancy Or Update Invariance:** investigate
   gauge-like structure from intrinsic equivalences or update invariances.
8. **Matter-Geometry Coupling:** test whether emergent fields can back-react on
   emergent geometry through a network-native source or conservation law.
9. **Standard Model Sector Attempt:** investigate whether recognizable charge,
   spin, chirality, interaction, and consistency structures can be recovered.
10. **Cosmology, Horizons, And Black-Hole Limits:** test large-scale regimes,
    causal boundaries, entropy-like behavior, and singularity handling.
11. **Discriminating Predictions And Benchmarks:** identify reproducible
    outcomes that differ from existing continuum and discrete approaches.
12. **Unified Field Theory Synthesis:** only after the earlier inputs close,
    synthesize the framework as a candidate unified field theory.

The immediate scientific next step is Paper 2: define the first admissible
higher-dimensional finite-capacity network class and the intrinsic observables
that would certify higher-dimensional spacetime-like recovery.

## Claim Boundary

We are not claiming that we are credentialed mathematicians or physicists, and
we are not asking anyone to accept results on authority. We are trying to make
the research artifacts legible enough that qualified reviewers can inspect,
reject, reproduce, or improve them.

As of now, this organization does not claim:

- a completed unified field theory;
- a physical proof that nature uses finite-capacity causal networks;
- recovered Einstein dynamics, matter fields, gauge fields, or quantum
  dynamics;
- peer-reviewed validation of the full program.

The intended standard is simple: ambitious goals are allowed, but claims should
move only when the supporting artifacts move.
